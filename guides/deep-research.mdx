## The Deep Research Mindset

**Surface research**: Ask one question, get one answer, done.

**Deep research**: Ask one question, analyze the answer, ask follow-ups, challenge assumptions, synthesize insights, validate findings, and build a comprehensive understanding.

**The key difference**: Deep research is a conversation, not a single query.

---

## The Deep Research Process

### Step 1: Start Broad
Begin with a big question to map the landscape.

### Step 2: Follow-Up with Specifics
Drill down into interesting areas that emerge.

### Step 3: Challenge and Validate
Question AI's responses, ask for sources of claims, test assumptions.

### Step 4: Look for Patterns
Ask AI to identify themes, contradictions, or connections across findings.

### Step 5: Synthesize Insights
Pull together all findings into actionable takeaways.

### Step 6: Verify Critical Points
Cross-check important facts and claims independently.

## Example: Deep Research in Action

### Surface-Level Research ❌
```
User: "What are jewellery trends for 2025?"
AI: [Gives list of 5-7 trends]
User: "Thanks!" [Stops there]
```

**Result**: Generic list. Everyone gets the same answer. No depth, no application to Annoushka.

---

### Deep Research ✅

**Question 1: Map the Landscape**
```
I need to research jewellery design trends for 2025 for Annoushka, a luxury British jewellery brand known for handcrafted pieces with personal meaning (lockets, charms, etc.).

Give me:
1. Top 5-7 macro trends in luxury jewellery for 2025
2. Brief explanation of each trend
3. Which demographics or customer segments are driving each trend
4. Examples of luxury brands embracing these trends

This is the foundation - I'll ask follow-ups to dig deeper.
```

**Question 2: Drill Into Relevance**
```
Of those trends you mentioned, which 3 are most relevant to Annoushka given:
- We specialize in sentimental, meaningful jewellery
- British craftsmanship is core to our brand
- Price points: £200-£5000
- Customer base: predominantly women 25-55, gift-givers

For each of the 3 most relevant trends, explain why they fit our brand and customer.
```

**Question 3: Competitive Analysis**
```
For [specific trend from above], how are these luxury brands approaching it:
- Monica Vinader
- Astley Clarke
- Links of London (similar market)

What are they doing well? What gaps or opportunities do you see for Annoushka to differentiate?
```

**Question 4: Challenge Assumptions**
```
You said [specific trend] is growing. What evidence supports this?

Are there any counter-trends or signs this might be peaking? What's the risk of following this trend too literally?
```

**Question 5: Application**
```
Based on everything we've discussed, give me 3 specific, actionable design direction recommendations for Annoushka's next collection that:
- Align with relevant trends
- Fit our brand DNA (sentimental, crafted, British)
- Differentiate from competitors
- Have commercial potential

For each recommendation, explain the rationale and potential risks.
```

**Question 6: Validation**
```
What research sources or industry reports would you recommend I consult to validate these findings? Where should I look for data on luxury jewellery market trends?
```

**Result**: Comprehensive, actionable research tailored to Annoushka, with a clear point of view and validated insights.

## Deep Research Techniques

### Technique 1: The Pyramid (Broad → Narrow)
Start with the biggest question, then narrow progressively.

**Example: Competitor Research**
```
Level 1 (Broad): "Give me an overview of [Competitor Brand]'s market positioning"
Level 2 (Narrower): "Focus on their product strategy - what categories, price points, and innovation approach"
Level 3 (Specific): "Analyze their charm bracelet collection specifically - how does it compare to Annoushka's?"
Level 4 (Application): "Based on this analysis, what opportunities exist for Annoushka to differentiate?"
```

---

### Technique 2: The Funnel (Question → Challenge → Synthesize)
Don't accept first answers. Push deeper.

**Example: Market Sizing**
```
Question: "What's the size of the UK luxury jewellery market?"
AI Response: "[Gives number]"

Challenge: "What's included in that figure? Is it retail or wholesale? Does it include ultra-luxury (£10k+) or just accessible luxury? What's the growth rate?"
AI Response: "[More detailed breakdown]"

Challenge again: "How reliable are these estimates? What sources are typically used for luxury market sizing? Where might this be over/understated?"

Synthesize: "Based on everything above, give me a realistic, conservative estimate of the addressable market for Annoushka specifically (our price range, our channels, our customer profile)"
```

---

### Technique 3: Multi-Perspective Analysis
Ask AI to analyze from different angles.

**Example: New Product Opportunity**
```
"Analyze the opportunity to launch [product type] from these perspectives:

1. Customer perspective: What need does this solve? Who wants this and why?
2. Competitive perspective: What's already available? How would we differentiate?
3. Brand fit perspective: Does this align with Annoushka's DNA?
4. Operational perspective: Can we actually make this at our quality standards?
5. Financial perspective: What's the investment and realistic return?

Be honest if perspectives conflict - that's valuable insight."
```

---

### Technique 4: Scenario Planning
Explore "what if" situations.

**Example: Strategic Decision**
```
"I'm considering [decision]. Model out 3 scenarios:

1. Best case: What happens if everything goes right? What enables this?
2. Base case: What's the most likely outcome? What drives this?
3. Worst case: What if it fails? Why might it fail?

For each scenario:
- Key assumptions
- Early warning signs to watch for
- How to maximize upside or mitigate downside
- Decision points (when to double down or cut losses)"
```

---

### Technique 5: Compare and Contrast
Use AI to find patterns across multiple examples.

**Example: Success Pattern Analysis**
```
"Analyze these 3 successful luxury jewellery brand launches:
1. [Brand A example]
2. [Brand B example]
3. [Brand C example]

Identify:
- Common success factors (what did they all do?)
- Key differences (how did they differentiate?)
- Patterns in their approach (timing, positioning, product strategy)
- Lessons for Annoushka (what's applicable vs not)

Present as: Patterns + Implications for us"
```

## Research Workflows for Common Tasks

### Workflow 1: Competitor Deep Dive

**Step 1: Landscape**
```
"Give me an overview of [Competitor]'s market positioning, brand strategy, and product portfolio. Focus on facts and observable elements."
```

**Step 2: Specifics**
```
"Now dig deeper into their [specific category - e.g., 'locket range']. What price points? What materials? What's the design aesthetic? How do they market it?"
```

**Step 3: Performance Indicators**
```
"Based on public information, what signals suggest this is performing well or poorly for them? Look at:
- Website prominence
- Social media engagement
- Customer reviews
- Retail distribution
- Any other visible indicators"
```

**Step 4: Strategic Insight**
```
"Given everything above:
1. What's their clear competitive advantage?
2. Where are they vulnerable?
3. How does Annoushka compare?
4. Where's the opportunity for us to differentiate or compete?"
```

**Step 5: Validation**
```
"What would you want to verify or research further to confirm these insights? What assumptions are we making that could be wrong?"
```

---

### Workflow 2: Market Trend Analysis

**Step 1: Identify**
```
"What are the major trends in [market/category] right now? Give me 7-10 trends with brief descriptions."
```

**Step 2: Validate**
```
"For each trend, tell me:
- Is this a real trend or just hype?
- What's the evidence (not just articles saying it's a trend, but actual behavior)?
- How long has it been emerging?
- Is it accelerating, peaking, or plateauing?"
```

**Step 3: Filter**
```
"Which of these trends are most relevant to luxury jewellery brands like Annoushka? Exclude trends that are:
- Mass market only
- Fashion/fast fashion focused
- Require technology we don't have
- Contradict luxury positioning"
```

**Step 4: Deep Dive**
```
[For top 3 relevant trends]:
"For [Trend X], give me:
- Examples of luxury brands doing this well
- How customers are responding
- How this could translate to Annoushka products specifically
- What we'd need to do to embrace this authentically
- Risks of getting it wrong"
```

**Step 5: Actionable Output**
```
"Summarize findings into a one-page brief:
- Top 3 trends for Annoushka to consider
- Specific product/design implications
- Examples and inspiration
- Recommendations for next steps"
```

---

### Workflow 3: Customer Insight Development

**Step 1: Define Segment**
```
"Help me understand [customer segment - e.g., 'women 30-45 who buy charm jewellery'].

What are their:
- Demographics (who they are)
- Psychographics (values, interests, lifestyle)
- Shopping behaviors (where, when, how they buy luxury)
- Motivations (why they buy jewellery, what they seek)"
```

**Step 2: Needs and Desires**
```
"What are this segment's unmet needs in luxury jewellery? What frustrates them about current options? What delights them? What trade-offs do they make?"
```

**Step 3: Purchase Drivers**
```
"For this segment, what drives purchase decisions?
- Rank these factors: Design, Craftsmanship, Brand, Price, Meaning/Story, Versatility, Trendiness
- What's the typical purchase journey?
- What influences them (people, media, events)?
- What's the decision timeline (impulse vs considered)?"
```

**Step 4: Application**
```
"Based on this profile, how should Annoushka:
- Talk to this customer (messaging, tone)
- Design for them (product attributes)
- Reach them (channels, tactics)
- Price for them (positioning)
- Service them (experience expectations)"
```

**Step 5: Validation**
```
"What would you need to validate about this customer profile through primary research? What assumptions should we test?"
```

---

### Workflow 4: Strategic Option Evaluation

**Step 1: Frame the Decision**
```
"I'm evaluating whether to [strategic option]. Help me structure the analysis.

Background: [context]
Options: [A vs B vs C]
Constraints: [time, budget, capabilities]

What factors should I be analyzing to make this decision well?"
```

**Step 2: Detailed Analysis**
```
[For each option]:
"Analyze [Option A] across these dimensions:
- Strategic fit (brand alignment)
- Market opportunity (size, growth, accessibility)
- Competitive advantage (can we win here?)
- Operational feasibility (can we actually do this?)
- Financial case (investment vs return)
- Risks (what could go wrong)
- Dependencies (what needs to be true)"
```

**Step 3: Scenario Modeling**
```
"For [preferred option], model:
- Best case (conservative assumptions)
- Most likely case
- Worst case

For each:
- Key assumptions
- Financial implications
- Strategic implications
- What we'd do in each scenario"
```

**Step 4: Decision Recommendation**
```
"Given all the above analysis:
1. What's your recommendation?
2. What's the rationale (top 3 reasons)?
3. What are the key risks and how to mitigate?
4. What would change your recommendation?
5. What should we do first (immediate next steps)?"
```

## Advanced Research Tactics

### Tactic 1: Teach AI, Then Ask
Prime AI with information, then use it.

**Example**:
```
"Here's Annoushka's brand positioning: [paste brand description, values, customer profile].

Here's our current product range: [describe].

Now, with that context, analyze [research question]."
```

**Why it works**: AI has your specific context, so answers are tailored not generic.

---

### Tactic 2: Multi-Source Synthesis
Use AI to synthesize across multiple sources.

**Example**:
```
"I'm going to share 3 articles about [topic].

Article 1: [paste or summarize]
Article 2: [paste or summarize]
Article 3: [paste or summarize]

Synthesize these into:
- Key themes (what do they all say?)
- Points of disagreement (where do they differ?)
- What's missing (gaps in coverage)
- Implications for Annoushka"
```

---

### Tactic 3: Red Team Your Ideas
Have AI argue against your thinking.

**Example**:
```
"I think we should [your proposal].

Now, act as a skeptical executive. Challenge this idea:
- What are the flaws in this thinking?
- What am I not seeing?
- What could go wrong?
- Why might this fail?
- What questions am I not asking?

Be tough - I need honest pushback."
```

---

### Tactic 4: Pattern Recognition
Ask AI to find patterns you might miss.

**Example**:
```
"Here's data on our top 20 performing products: [data]

Analyze and identify:
- Patterns in what performs well (commonalities)
- Anomalies (things that break the pattern)
- Correlations (what attributes associate with success)
- Hypotheses about what drives performance
- Tests we could run to validate hypotheses"
```

---

### Tactic 5: Role-Play Perspectives
Have AI inhabit different perspectives.

**Example**:
```
"Analyze [topic] from the perspective of:
1. The CEO (strategic lens)
2. The customer (value lens)
3. The designer (creative lens)
4. The CFO (financial lens)
5. The store manager (operational lens)

For each perspective, what do they care about? What questions would they ask? Where might they disagree with each other?"
```

## When to Stop Researching and Decide

Deep research can go on forever. Know when to stop:

### Stop when you have:
✅ Enough information to make a confident decision
✅ Diminishing returns (new questions aren't adding much)
✅ Identified what needs validation through action (not more research)
✅ Clear next steps that don't require more desk research

### Red flags you're over-researching:
🚩 Procrastinating on a decision you're uncomfortable making
🚩 Seeking certainty that doesn't exist
🚩 Researching details that don't change the answer
🚩 Asking the same question different ways hoping for a different answer

**Remember**: Research informs decisions, it doesn't make them. At some point, human judgment must take over.

## Verifying AI Research

**AI is great at synthesis, patterns, and generating ideas. AI is terrible at facts and current information.**

### Always verify:
- Statistics and data points (check sources)
- Claims about specific companies or products
- Market sizing and forecasts
- Technical specifications
- Legal or regulatory information
- Anything that would be consequential if wrong

### How to verify:
- **Cross-reference**: Check multiple sources
- **Go to primary sources**: Company websites, official reports, academic research
- **Use AI to suggest sources**: "Where would I find reliable data on this?"
- **Sanity check**: Does this pass the common sense test?
- **Ask experts**: Consult people who actually know the space

**Rule of thumb**: If you're going to make a significant decision based on it, verify it independently.

## Common Research Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Accepting First Answers
**Problem**: First response is usually generic.
**Solution**: Always ask follow-ups. Go three levels deep.

### Mistake 2: Not Providing Context
**Problem**: AI gives generic answers because it doesn't know your specific situation.
**Solution**: Start with context about Annoushka, your customer, your constraints.

### Mistake 3: Treating AI as Source of Truth
**Problem**: AI can confidently state incorrect information.
**Solution**: Verify facts. Use AI for synthesis and ideas, not factual authority.

### Mistake 4: Not Challenging AI
**Problem**: Accept answers without questioning assumptions or logic.
**Solution**: Ask "What could be wrong with this?" "What am I not seeing?"

### Mistake 5: Research Without Application
**Problem**: Collect information without clear purpose or next steps.
**Solution**: Always end with "So what?" and "Now what?"

## Quick Reference: Deep Research Checklist

Before you finish research, ask yourself:

- [ ] Have I explored this from multiple angles?
- [ ] Have I challenged the initial findings?
- [ ] Have I identified patterns and connections?
- [ ] Have I considered what could be wrong with this analysis?
- [ ] Have I applied findings to Annoushka specifically?
- [ ] Have I identified what needs further validation?
- [ ] Do I have actionable recommendations?
- [ ] Have I verified critical facts independently?
- [ ] Can I make a decision with this information?
- [ ] Do I know what to do next?

If you answered no to several of these, you're not done researching deeply.
